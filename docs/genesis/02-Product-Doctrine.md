# Chapter 2 — Product Doctrine

This chapter records the frozen product rules used to judge implementation.

## Simplicity

> The complexity of software depends on the simplicity of its solution.

The system may model deep production knowledge, but the interface must expose only the context needed for the current work.

## No use. No see.

Unused, unavailable, unlicensed, or contextually irrelevant functionality stays out of view. This applies to modules, properties, controls, AI, animation, and pricing entitlements.

## Explain complex knowledge

Filminex makes complex knowledge explainable. It does not merely store isolated records. Relationships, inheritance, source, and impact must remain discoverable.

## Property-based control

Stable production knowledge is represented through explicit properties and relationships. Free-form observations and prompts do not replace structured data.

AI can propose property values. Users remain able to inspect, correct, override, and lock them.

## Explain before automate

Automation must not hide where a value or decision came from. Effective values expose provenance and provide a direct path to their source.

## AI is optional

Core filmmaking workflows operate without AI or animation subscriptions. AI Studio is an add-on capability, not the identity of the product.

## Context is the interface

The workspace, current entity, selected row, and active craft determine what the user sees. A consistent inspector is preferred to proliferating popups and disconnected forms.
